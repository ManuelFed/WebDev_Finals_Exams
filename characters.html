ANTAGONIST

Sid Phillips

“☠️Toys were made to be destroyed☠️”

He is the mischievous, destructive neighbor boy known for tearing apart toys and rebuilding them into frightening creations. In Toy Story, he serves as the primary enemy, driven by curiosity, chaos, and a love of explosions. He is imaginative but reckless, treating toys as experiments rather than companions. His actions create the central conflict for Woody and Buzz, ultimately leading to the moment when the toys reveal they are alive to teach him a lesson.

  • Origin: He is a young boy living in the suburban neighborhood next door to Andy Davis. He comes from a typical middle-class family and shares his home with his parents and his younger sister, Hannah. Growing up surrounded by toys, tools, and whatever he can get his hands on, Sid developed a fascination with taking things apart and experimenting—often in destructive ways. His environment is normal, but his behavior stands out because of his chaotic imagination and love for “explosive creativity.”

  • Abilities: Highly imaginative, Skilled at taking toys apart and rebuilding them into new forms, Good with tools, gadgets, and makeshift inventions, Fearless when experimenting with explosions, fireworks, and mechanics

  • Weakness: Reckless and unaware of consequences, Easily frightened when confronted with something unexpected, Lack of empathy for others, including toys, Curiosity can lead him into dangerous or trouble-making situations

SUPPORTING CHARACTERS

Bo Peep

Bo Peep is a gentle, porcelain shepherdess doll who appears as Woody’s close friend and love interest. She originally belongs to Molly’s bedside lamp and often acts as the calm, supportive voice among the toys. With her soft spoken charm, playful teasing, and steady confidence in Woody, she helps reassure the other toys during tense moments. In the first film, Bo Peep is portrayed as elegant, kind, and quietly brave always offering comfort and showing unwavering faith in Woody.

Mr. Potato

SMr. Potato Head is a sarcastic, quick-witted toy with removable facial features and limbs. In Toy Story, he’s known for his sharp humor, expressive personality, and frequent playful arguments especially with Hamm and the other toys. Despite his grumpy streak, he’s loyal and proves he cares about his friends when it matters.

Slinky Dog

Slinky Dog, or “Slinky,” is a loyal and good natured toy dachshund with a stretchable metal spring for a body. He’s one of Woody’s closest friends, often offering support, calm reasoning, and optimism. In this movie, Slinky stands out for his kindness, playful humor, and unwavering trust in Woody—always trying to keep the group together.

Hamm

Hamm is a wisecracking piggy bank toy known for his dry humor and sarcastic remarks. In Toy Story, he often delivers clever one liners and acts as one of the more logical, down to earth members of the group. Though he jokes a lot, Hamm is smart, observant, and always ready to help the other toys when it counts.

Rex

Rex is a large green plastic dinosaur with a big heart and constant worries. Despite his fierce appearance, he’s gentle, anxious, and often afraid he’s not scary enough. In Toy Story, Rex is lovable for his nervous energy, enthusiasm, and eagerness to please, always trying his best to help his friends even when he doubts himself.

Sarge

Sarge is the disciplined and fearless leader of the Green Army Men. In Toy Story, he commands his troops with strict military precision and unwavering loyalty to Andy. Known for his bravery and tactical skills, Sarge leads important reconnaissance missions and always puts the safety and success of the toys first.

Andy Davis

Andy Davis is the cheerful, imaginative boy who owns Woody, Buzz, and the rest of the toys. In Toy Story, he’s shown as kind hearted and creative, often bringing his toys to life through energetic play. Although he doesn't appear often on screen, Andy’s love for his toys is what drives the story—especially Woody’s loyalty and the emotional bond shared among the toys.

Hannah Phillips

Hannah Phillips is Sid’s younger sister, a sweet and gentle girl who loves playing with her dolls. Despite living with Sid’s chaotic pranks, she stays kind-hearted and patient. In Toy Story, Hannah becomes a contrast to Sid’s destructive behavior showing that toys can be loved, cared for, and played with the way they’re meant to be.
